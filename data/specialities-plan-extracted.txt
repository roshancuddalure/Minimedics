 
 `r`n Mednecta Medical Taxonomy Implementation Document `r`n 1. Purpose `r`n The Mednecta Medical Taxonomy is a structured classification system used to organize all medical disciplines, specialties, and subspecialties within the platform. `r`n This taxonomy supports multiple core features of the Mednecta ecosystem including: `r`n User profile specialization `r`n Content categorization `r`n Quiz tagging `r`n Specialty leaderboards `r`n Expert discovery `r`n Research collaboration matching `r`n Advanced search filters `r`n Future medical knowledge graph architecture `r`n The taxonomy is designed to be hierarchical and scalable to support expansion as the platform grows. `r`n `r`n 2. Taxonomy Hierarchy Structure `r`n The taxonomy consists of three hierarchical levels. `r`n Level 1 — Domain `r`n A broad medical field or discipline. `r`n Example: `r`n Internal Medicine `r`n Surgery `r`n Pediatrics `r`n Level 2 — Specialty `r`n A major professional field within a domain. `r`n Example: `r`n Cardiology `r`n Orthopedic Surgery `r`n Radiology `r`n Level 3 — Subspecialty / Topic Tag `r`n Focused areas within a specialty used for detailed categorization. `r`n Example: `r`n Interventional Cardiology `r`n Spine Surgery `r`n Neuroimaging `r`n `r`n 3.
